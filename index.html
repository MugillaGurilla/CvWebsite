About

QA Automation Engineer

I currently work for Immediate Media, a publishing house that produces content for brands like BBC Good Food and Radio Times. Specifically, I'm part of the Product & Tech team, managing the Content Management System for all our brands.

UI Automation Testing:

The largest part of my job is writing new and improving old automation UI tests. These tests run in Immediate pipelines as part of CI/CD.

Unit Tests:

I also write some unit tests for both front end and back end services.

API Testing:

Another aspect of my job is writing automated API tests, these tests utilise Postman but are technically written with a bespoke CLI package that was developed in-house.

Finding & Reporting Defects:

I come across bugs regularly. I’ll either reach out to the team whose responsibility that the defect is or write a high quality defect if it’s my own team’s responsibility.

Shaping Tickets:

Tickets are rarely Ready for Development when initially written so my team has regular shaping sessions to discuss, alter and story point tickets. The contribution of QAs in these sessions is very important to make sure the risk, impact, scope and acceptance criteria of the ticket is as it should be, and understood by all. These sessions are QAs time to ensure that the ticket is as it should be.

Holistic Understanding:

A very part of my job is having a holistic understanding of the CMS. QAs must understand everything from product perspectives totting considerations to resourcing to in-house Subject Matter Experts. If developers are the gears, QAs are the grease that makes everything run smoothly.

Communication:

Unfortunately, if I’m talking with someone it is usually because something is wrong. This can be difficult because, well, humans are humans, so interpersonal and communication skills are absolutely crucial.

Test Automation Engineer

I was recently engaged by a Digital Asset Management company based in Singapore. They asked myself and a few others on the team to create a framework and a suite of tests to cover their web-based trading platform.

Project Management:

I focused on project management, gathering requirements and creating user stories and ensuring the team runs smoothly.

Java:

I am trained in C# but the tests were written in Java meaning I had to quickly familiarise myself with Java.

Auth0 and Authentication:

The main blocker was automating login and working around auth0 authentication, being able to log into the portal in a repeatable way. Each login had to be authenticated from a email sent to the account holder's address. It was difficult to workaround but this was eventually done by accessing gmail's API.

Scrum and Changing Requirements:

The requirements of this project were constantly changing. As such, it's very important for the team to be agile. To achieve this, we're organising the team using a Scrum methodology.

Communication with Clients:

Clear communication with the client was important and to ensure this, we have designated a single person to relay messages to and from the client.

Documentation:

The members of the team come and go, as a result, the documentation for the project needs to be detailed, up-to-date and accurate. This enables people to quickly and easily get up to speed with the project.

Assistant Brewer

I worked for nearly two years as brewer. I spent time at three different breweries but most of my time was with Sambrook's in Wandsworth, which you can see pictured beside (I'm the guy up the ladder).

QC and QA:

Ensuring the quality of the product is of paramount importance so that someone new to the product will come back for more. This was the raison d'etre for an assisant brewer - making sure the product gets to the customer as good as possible.

Packaging:

Doing this properly is the main way to ensure quality. Oxygen or other contaminants should stay within tolerance ranges.

Small Batch Brewing:

I did get to make some experimental beers on the test kit.

Prioritisation:

Goals are meant to be set long in advance but it rarely worked out this way. Often I’d hear in the morning that something needs to be ready for the afternoon and have to ensure that it is ready.

Documentation:

As with all food products, documentation and analysis of the product from grain to glass is important. This takes the form of taking and recording pH, gravity (a measure of sugar within the product), oxygen levels, CO2 levels and clarity.

Data Migration Analyst

I worked as a Data Migration Analyst before moving into brewing. I was employed by Lex Consultancy but worked on site with Canada Life.

Accuracy:

It was very important to be accurate with regard to punctuation, grammar and typography errors. Correct data in the now saves a lot of work in the future

Attention-to-Detail:

Spotting the smallest mistakes was crucial to this role. Not spotting them could cause a lot of headaches down the line.

Teamwork:

It was project and team-oriented work. As with everything, communication is key to project work. During this role, I became a defacto Team Lead for my team of three.

Prioritisation:

There were always long term goals but at any moment, higher priority could come in taking precedence.
